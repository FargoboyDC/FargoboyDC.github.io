How we got started

Sophie started baking from an early age with her mom. She has baked and sold cakes and other products. Sophie has always wanted to go into social work for as long as she can remember and she had always thought about combining baking with this because she has used baking as a time to relax and step back. She went to Camp Daybreak which is a summer camp that works with people affected by disabilities and has volunteered there for the past two years.

We wanted to run baking classes with individuals with mental disabilities in brattleboro that were self-funded. We felt that self-funding was important because in working with these individuals we often develop close bonds, and we needed to be sure that we could consistently offer our program. We felt that we could better utilize our resources We decided that we wanted to integrate our sustainability with our baking classes. We are going to run five weeks of classes with the fifth week being a bake sale whose proceeds will fund the next set of classes.
